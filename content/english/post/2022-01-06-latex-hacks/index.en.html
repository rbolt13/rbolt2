Here is some short and simple latex that I often find helpful.

Basic Symbols :

  • \(\sim\) : \sim

  • \(\circ\) : \circ

  • \(\square\) : \square

  • \(\equiv\) : \equiv

  • \(\div\) : \div

  • \(a\choose b\) : a\choose b

  • \(\mathbb{R}\) : \mathbb{R}

    • bb : blackboard bold

Inline :

Limits above and below sums and integrals

  • \(\sum\limits_{n}^{i}\int_0^1\) : \limits

Matrices and Matrix Equations

  • \(\begin{smallmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1\end{smallmatrix}\) : \{smallmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1\end{smallmatrix}

    • \(I=[\begin{smallmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1\end{smallmatrix}]\)

    • \((\begin{smallmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0\end{smallmatrix})(\begin{smallmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1\end{smallmatrix})\ne(\begin{smallmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1\end{smallmatrix})(\begin{smallmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0\end{smallmatrix})\)

Multiple Lines :

Function

  • \(F(x)=\begin{cases}1 & x\geq 0\\0 & \text{otherwise}\end{cases}\) : F(x)=\begin{cases} . . . \end{cases}

    • 1 & x \geq 0 \\

    • 0 & \text{otherwise}

Series of Equalities

  • \(\begin{equation}\label{a}\begin{split}x &= a+b+c\\&=1+2+3\\&=6\end{split}\end{equation}\) : `\[\begin{equation}\{label}\begin{split}... \end{split}\end{equation}\]’

    • x &= a+b+c \\

    • &= 1+2+3

    • &= 6

Advice

Google Docs Equations Boxes

  • Most latex backslashes work in google doc’s equation boxes. If I have to do a “quick” homework, and dont want to spend a lot of time formatting a pdf in R, I will use google docs, and these shortcuts in the equations boxes.

    • Note that before I started using R this is how I began learning latex.
